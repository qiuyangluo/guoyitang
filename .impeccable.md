## Design Context

### Users
The primary audience is local Chinese-speaking families in Flushing, New York, roughly ages 30 to 80. They are often evaluating care for themselves, their parents, spouses, or children, and they need a clinic website that makes important information easy to trust and easy to act on. Their main job to be done is to quickly confirm that the clinic is legitimate, experienced, welcoming, nearby, and suitable for common concerns such as pain, sleep, women's health, men's health, recovery, and general body regulation.

### Brand Personality
The brand should feel credible, traditional, and welcoming. The emotional goals are trusted medical authority and calm healing. The tone should communicate long-term experience, steady hands, and community familiarity rather than trendiness or entertainment.

### Aesthetic Direction
The preferred direction is light mode with an editorial, traditional Chinese medicine sensibility: warm parchment tones, jade-inflected greens, restrained gold accents, and composed layouts that feel established rather than flashy. Typography should feel refined and authoritative. The site should not feel playful, gimmicky, or youth-branded.

### Design Principles
1. Lead with trust before decoration: surface experience, location, credentials, and access clearly.
2. Make calm feel intentional: use restrained motion, warm spacing, and soft contrast rather than loud effects.
3. Preserve traditional character: draw from herbal, apothecary, and clinic cues without becoming old-fashioned or cluttered.
4. Design for family decision-making: keep calls to action obvious, phone-first, and reassuring for multi-generational users.
5. Avoid playful styling: no cute illustrations, novelty interactions, or overly trendy visual tropes.
